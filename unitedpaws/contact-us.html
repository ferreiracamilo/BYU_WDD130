Jilt/Loss/Abuse

  • Castration to avoid overpopulation and trafficking
  • You must always have the leash on and you must hold it firmly when you go out with your pet
  • In case of not being able to pay for chronic treatment of your pet, contact NGOs or veterinary schools
  • Adoption should be your first option and avoid clandestine sites
  • Don't adopt more pets than you can care for
  • You must follow the COTRIVA regulations and animal law
  • Do not assist, encourage, or participate in animal fights
  • If you find a lost animal, try to help it and/or contact an animal organization.

Responsible Caring

  • Your pet must have veterinary coverage and frequent check-ups
  • If your pet is big during the walks must wear a muzzle
  • Make sure your pet eats balanced food
  • During holiday eves take extra precautions to prevent your pet from escaping
  • Any space such as a garden or backyard should have a good quality fence
  • Size of your home must be commensurate with the size of your pet
  • Castration prevents and facilitates detection of benign diseases
  • Provide fresh water and food to your pet

Miscellaneous

  • During the summer leave a water jar for stray animals
  • During the winter, leave food and free spaces that can serve as temporary shelter for stray animals
  • When walking your pet, check that the place admits animals
  • When walking your pet, you should always pick up their excrement.
  • Avoid taking your pet to crowded places to avoid unexpected scenarios and stress in them
  • Check that your pet's leash or harness supports its weight
  • In case of moving, it is recommended to transport them after having finished the move.
  • Educate your pet to be sociable and friendly
